## Geometric Flavor from an Extra Dimension

A unified geometric parametrization of fermion masses and mixings based on a compact extra dimension with \(S^1/Z_2 \times Z_3\) symmetry.

### Key ideas
- **Geometry as the origin of flavor:** Three generations are localized at \(Z_3\)-symmetric points on an orbifolded circle. Yukawa couplings arise from the overlap of Gaussian wavefunctions with a scalar vacuum profile \(\phi(\theta)=a_0+R\cos(\theta+\phi)\).

### Main results
- **Koide relation** becomes the geometric condition \(R=\sqrt{2}a_0\), derived in three lines from \(Z_3\) sampling identities.
- **Cabibbo angle** is linked to the light-quark mass ratio: \(|V_{us}|\approx\sqrt{m_u/m_c}\) — a parameter-free consequence of warp-factor cancellation.
- **Neutrino masses** follow an odd Kaluza–Klein sine profile, predicting \(\sum m_\nu\approx 0.115\ \mathrm{eV}\).
- **Large PMNS mixing** arises from small localization shifts, in direct analogy to the quark sector.
- **CP violation** in both sectors is sourced by a single Wilson-line phase \(e^{iq\theta}\).

### Numerical validation
A full numerical scan of the Yukawa structure reproduces the light-quark masses and CKM elements with high accuracy. The top and charm masses are underestimated, indicating that the simplest warp profile requires refinement.

### Status
The model is best interpreted as a **compact, testable geometric parametrization** rather than a final ultraviolet theory. It makes several falsifiable predictions and is accompanied by fully reproducible Python code.
